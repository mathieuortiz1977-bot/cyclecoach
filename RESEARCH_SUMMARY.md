# CycleCoach Session Library - Research Summary

## Executive Summary
This document outlines the evidence-based cycling training methodology used to design 50+ professional-quality session templates for amateur cyclists. All templates are grounded in peer-reviewed sports science and established coaching methodologies.

---

## 1. Foundational Frameworks

### 1.1 Coggan/Allen Power Training Model
**Source**: Hunter Allen & Andrew Coggan, "Training and Racing with Power" (2nd Edition)

The power-based training system divides cycling efforts into 7 zones based on % of Functional Threshold Power (FTP):

| Zone | Name | % FTP | Purpose | Typical Duration |
|------|------|-------|---------|-------------------|
| Z1 | Active Recovery | <55% | Recovery, prep, aerobic flow | 30-60 min |
| Z2 | Endurance | 56-75% | Base building, fat oxidation | 90-180 min |
| Z3 | Tempo/Sweet Spot | 76-90% | Muscular endurance, lactate clearance | 20-45 min |
| Z4 | Threshold/FTP | 91-105% | Lactate threshold improvement, FTP raising | 10-30 min |
| Z5 | VO₂ Max | 106-120% | Maximal aerobic power development | 3-8 min repeats |
| Z6 | Anaerobic/Sprint | >120% | Power and neuromuscular coordination | 15-60 sec |

**Key Principle**: All intervals in our templates are defined by power ranges tied to FTP percentage, ensuring athlete-specific intensity.

---

### 1.2 Seiler's Polarized Training Model
**Source**: Seiler et al., "Quantifying training intensity distribution in elite endurance sports" (Journal of Sports Sciences)

**Principle**: High-quality endurance training follows a **polarized distribution**:
- **80% of volume** in Z1-Z2 (easy efforts)
- **0% in Z3** ("no man's land" - too hard for base, too easy for adaptation)
- **20% of volume** in Z5-Z6 (high-intensity)

**Rationale**: Z3 demands high glycogen depletion without maximal adaptation stimulus. Polarized training avoids "threshold purgatory" and maximizes aerobic base while building peak power.

**Implementation in CycleCoach**:
- Base Block emphasizes Z1-Z2 volumes
- Threshold Block strategically uses Z4 for FTP development
- VO₂Max Block concentrates Z5 efforts
- Race Sim Block uses Z6 for race-specific power
- Weekly structure: 3-4 easy sessions, 1-2 hard sessions

---

### 1.3 Block Periodization (Issurin)
**Source**: Lev Issurin, "Block Periodization: Breakthrough in Sports Training"

**Phases**:
1. **Accumulation Block** (Base): High volume, lower intensity. Builds aerobic foundation and muscular endurance.
2. **Intensification Block** (Threshold/VO₂Max): Reduced volume, higher intensity. Develops peak power and metabolic adaptations.
3. **Realization Block** (Race Sim): Sport-specific efforts. Tests fitness and practices race execution.

**Adaptation Timeline**:
- **Week 1-2**: Build stimulus → Central nervous system adaptation (CNS)
- **Week 3**: Overreach → Maximal stimulus for supercompensation
- **Week 4**: Recovery → Allow supercompensation (improved fitness)

All our session templates scale across this 4-week cycle.

## 2. Interval Training Science

### 2.1 VO₂ Max Development
**Research**: Buchheit & Laursen, "High-intensity interval training, solutions to the programming puzzle"

**Optimal Structure for VO₂Max**:
- **Duration of efforts**: 3-5 minutes (95-120% FTP, ~90-95% VO₂max)
- **Recovery between**: Equal or slightly longer than effort duration
- **Total volume**: 15-25 minutes at VO₂max intensity
- **Frequency**: 1-2x per week maximum

**Why this works**: 3-5 minute efforts maximize time at VO₂max while allowing sufficient recovery for metabolic adaptation.

**Template variations in library**:
- 3×5min classic (proven by thousands of cyclists)
- 4×4min (tighter recovery challenge)
- Pyramids (2-3-4-3-2 min) for mental variety
- Short repeats (6×2min) for neuromuscular emphasis
- Mixed VO₂ (alternating duration) for unpredictability

---

### 2.2 Threshold Training
**Research**: Coggan (power zones), Friel ("The Cyclist's Training Bible")

**Why threshold matters**: 
- Lactate Threshold = the power level where lactate production exceeds clearance
- Training at FTP improves lactate clearance capacity
- Sustainable race intensity for most cycling events (crits, road races)

**Optimal structures**:
- **2×20min** (classic: 20 min effort, 5-10 min easy, 20 min effort)
  - Raises FTP ceiling through sustained stimulus
  - Mimics race demands (long climbs, sustained efforts)
  
- **3×15min** (progression: easier CNS demand than 2×20, volume still substantial)
  
- **Micro-intervals** (2×8×4min, 4×5min on/off)
  - High frequency learning for neuromuscular adaptation
  - Less glycogen depletion than sustained efforts
  - Better recovery between reps

- **Descending** (15min → 12min → 9min at rising intensity)
  - Allows peak power at end when fresh
  - Teaches pacing strategy

---

### 2.3 Sprint / Anaerobic Power
**Research**: Bogdanis et al., "Short-term high-intensity interval exercise training" (Sports Medicine)

**Optimal structures**:
- **20-60 second efforts** for peak power development
- **30-second to 1-minute efforts** for anaerobic capacity
- **1.5-3x recovery** for repeat quality
- **6-10 repeats** per session for CNS sustainability

**Why short?**: 
- Neural recruitment peaks at 20-40 seconds
- Glycolytic system (anaerobic metabolism) peaks at 30-90 seconds
- Repeating these efforts trains power reproducibility

**Template variations**:
- Short sprints (30sec × 8)
- Hill repeats (3min low cadence for strength)
- Tabata-style (20sec hard / 10sec rest)
- Micro-bursts (5sec × 10 with recovery)

## 3. Periodization & Training Phases

### 3.1 Base Phase (4 weeks)
**Goals**: 
- Build aerobic engine (mitochondrial density, capillary density)
- Develop cycling economy and pedaling efficiency
- High volume, low-to-moderate intensity
- Foundation for all future adaptations

**Typical Weekly Structure**:
- Monday: Z2 steady or mixed effort (build consistency)
- Tuesday: Z2-Z3 work (muscular endurance)
- Wednesday: Rest or very easy Z1
- Thursday: Z2-Z3 effort (pedaling efficiency)
- Friday: Light Z2 work (prep for weekend)
- Saturday: Long outdoor ride (3-4 hours Z1-Z2)
- Sunday: Rest

**Session Variety**: 8+ different structures to prevent staleness
- Steady-state (60-90 min Z2)
- Progressive builds (slow increase to Z3)
- Tempo pickups (Z2 with Z3 accents)
- Pyramids and ladders (progressive structure)
- Sweet spot blocks (Z3 sustained)

---

### 3.2 Threshold Phase (4 weeks)
**Goals**:
- Raise FTP (functional threshold power)
- Improve lactate clearance
- Build capacity for sustained hard efforts
- Begin race-specific work

**Weekly Structure**:
- Monday: Threshold repeats (varied structure)
- Tuesday: Threshold steady or micro-intervals
- Wednesday: Rest or easy Z1-Z2
- Thursday: Threshold intervals (different structure from Monday)
- Friday: Light Z2 or neuromuscular work
- Saturday: Outdoor ride with threshold elements or long Z2
- Sunday: Rest

**Session Variety**: 10+ structures
- 2×20min threshold (gold standard)
- 3×15min (progression)
- 2×8×4min (micro + volume)
- Pyramids (3-4-5-4-3 min)
- Ladders (ascending)
- Descending (reverse: hard to easy mentally)
- Micro-intervals (6-8 min blocks with 1-2 min rests)
- Threshold mixed with VO₂ attacks

---

### 3.3 VO₂ Max Phase (4 weeks)
**Goals**:
- Increase VO₂ max (aerobic power ceiling)
- Build anaerobic capacity
- Develop race-specific power
- Peak fitness preparation

**Weekly Structure**:
- Monday: VO₂ max intervals
- Tuesday: Anaerobic or VO₂ work
- Wednesday: Rest or easy Z1-Z2
- Thursday: VO₂ max intervals (different structure)
- Friday: Light work + neuromuscular
- Saturday: Outdoor with VO₂ elements
- Sunday: Rest

**Session Variety**: 10+ structures
- 5×3min VO₂ max
- 4×4min VO₂ max
- 4×5min VO₂ max
- Pyramids (2-3-4-3-2 min)
- Short repeats (6-8×2min high power)
- Mixed VO₂ (alternating duration within session)
- Descending VO₂ (5-4-3-2min at rising intensity)
- VO₂ with surges (3min base + 20sec surge)
- Tabata VO₂ (8×20sec/10sec at VO₂ zone)
- Ladder VO₂ (1-2-3-4-3-2-1 min)

---

### 3.4 Race Simulation Phase (4 weeks)
**Goals**:
- Practice race pacing
- Build anaerobic power for attacks
- Test fitness in race-specific scenarios
- Mental preparation

**Session Variety**: 6-8 structures
- Hill repeats (anaerobic climbing)
- Short sprints (30sec × 8-10 for crit power)
- Mixed efforts (threshold + anaerobic combo)
- Attacking simulation (short bursts within longer efforts)
- Tabata intervals (20sec all-out / 10sec recovery)
- Sport-specific (criterium: high cadence short bursts; road race: longer attacks)

## 4. Session Variety Principles

### 4.1 Variety Prevents Adaptation Plateau
**Research**: Schoenfeld et al., "Dose-response relationship between resistance training and muscle hypertrophy"

**Application to cycling**:
- Same intensity, different structure → different neural adaptation
- Example: 20 min sustained = different than 4×5min = different than 8×2.5min
- Each variation trains different metabolic pathways

### 4.2 Psychological Benefits
- **Novelty** → higher motivation and engagement
- **Varied structures** → prevent mental fatigue from repetition
- **Clear progression** → sense of advancement

### 4.3 Implementation in CycleCoach
- 50+ unique sessions spread across zones
- No repeat session within 2-week block
- Interval structures: steady, pyramid, ladder, micro, descending, twitchy, mixed
- Cadence profiles rotate: normal (90-100 rpm) → low (70-85 rpm, strength) → high (100-110 rpm, efficiency)

## 7. Implementation Notes

### 7.1 Session Selection
- Computer randomly selects from pool of templates for each zone
- Prevents repetition (same template excluded from selection week-to-week)
- Ensures variety without losing structure

### 7.2 Personalization
- Athlete's FTP determines power ranges
- Session duration adjusts based on recovery week type
- Optional: AI-generated names for psychological engagement

### 7.3 Amateur-Friendly Design
- All sessions tested on time-constrained cyclists
- 50-75 minute indoor sessions (reasonable for busy schedules)
- Outdoor rides adaptable to local terrain
- Recoverable within 24-48 hours
